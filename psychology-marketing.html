Psychology & Marketing

How my UCL psychology training and my work at Heyr shape the way I think about campaigns, content, and growth.

Studying psychology at UCL trained me to look at behaviour before tactics. In marketing, that means asking why people do what they do before deciding which channel or format to use.

At Heyr, I translate that into concrete decisions: how we frame value, what objections we address, and how we reduce friction in the journey from first contact to active member.

From theory to campaigns

  • Cognitive biases: using concepts like loss aversion or social proof responsibly, to help users make confident choices rather than pressure them.
  • Motivation & habits: understanding what keeps people engaged over time, and how to design touchpoints that support long-term use instead of short spikes.
  • Research mindset: treating campaigns as experiments, not just executions, and always looking for the next question the data suggests.

Why this matters for marketing

A psychology + data-driven approach helps me create campaigns that feel human, not generic: matching messaging to real needs, choosing the right moment to talk to users, and measuring what actually changes behaviour instead of only chasing vanity metrics.

The result is work that aims to be both effective for the business and respectful of the people on the other side of the screen.
